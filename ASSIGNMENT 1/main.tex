	\textbf{Problem 3.12(c)} Sketch the pole-zero plot for 
	\begin{align}
	    X_3(n)= \frac {1 + z^{-1} - 2z^{-2}} {1 - 13/6 z^{-1} + z^{2}}
	\end{align}
	and shade the region of convergence?\\
	
	\solution
	Given that, 
	
	\begin{align}
	    X_3(n)= \frac {1 + z^{-1} - 2z^{-2}}{1 - 13/6 z^{-1} + z^{2}}
	\end{align}
	As we know that, the numerator gives us the zeros and denominator gives the poles of a Z-transform.
	
	\begin{align}
	1 + z^{-1} - 2z^{-2} = 0
	\end{align}
	On solving, we get the zeros are 1 and -2.\linebreak
	Similarly, 
	
	\begin{align}
    	1 - 13/6 z^{-1} + z^{2} = 0
	\end{align}  
	On solving, we get the poles are 2/3 and 3/2. \\
	Since the $x_3(n)$ is absolutely summable, the ROC must include the unit circle: 
	$ \frac{2}{3} < \abs{x} < \frac{3}{2} $\\
